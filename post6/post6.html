Generation Z and Alpha: Real Dates (Mathematical & Gregorian & Sociological rationale)

Gregorian calendar key point

The Gregorian calendar has no year 0 — 1 BCE is followed immediately by 1 CE — therefore counting years is done on the natural numbers starting from 1. This affects how fixed-length blocks (like 15-year generations) map onto calendar years.

Generational concept used here

Generations are defined as strictly 15-year blocks. Each block is inclusive of its end year. Using arithmetic progression with a block length (d) of 15 yields end years that are divisible by 15 (when counting from year 1).

Arithmetic progression check (example using 2010)

  1. Take 2010 and divide by 15: 2010 ÷ 15 = 134 remainder 0.
  2. Because there is no remainder, 2010 is exactly the last year of a 15-year block.
  3. Therefore 31 Dec 2010 is the end of one generation block (Gen Z in this framework).

Conclusion: the block that ends in 2010 runs from 1 Jan 1996 to 31 Dec 2010 (Gen Z). The next block (Gen Alpha) therefore runs 1 Jan 2011 to 31 Dec 2025.
