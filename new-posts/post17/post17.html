Census of India – Timeline, 2011 Dataset, and the Upcoming 2027 Census:

Since 1871, a census has been conducted in India on the first year of every decade of the Gregorian calendar. The census years have been: 1871–72, 1881, 1891, 1901, 1911, 1921, 1931, 1941, 1951, 1961, 1971, 1981, 1991, 2001, and the latest completed census in 2011.
There was also supposed to be a census conducted in 2021. However, it was cancelled due to the COVID-19 pandemic. Because of this delay, the Census of India 2011 remained the longest-utilised official dataset for an extended period.

Census 2011 – Data Collection and Reference Date:

The data collection for Census 2011 was carried out between 9 February and 28 February 2011, with the reference date set as 1 March 2011 for the dataset.
The first dataset released was the provisional total of the country, which included:
  1. Total population of India
  2. Male population and female population
  3. Population of all States and Union Territories
  4. Decadal growth rate (2001–2011)
  5. Sex ratio
  6. Population density
  7. Rural and urban population distribution
  8. Share of each state in the total population
  9. Basic state-wise rankings and comparisons with the 2001 census
  10. Preliminary literacy rate
  11. Key summary demographic indicators
This provisional dataset was released on 31 March 2011.

Delay and Shift in Census Cycle:

The Census 2011 was also the last census conducted strictly in the pattern of being held in the first year of the decade. This pattern changed due to the delay caused by the cancellation of the 2021 census. As a result, the next census is planned for 2027, and it is expected to be followed by 2037, rather than 2031 as per the earlier cycle. The enumeration phase data for the upcoming Census 2027 is expected to be collected in February 2027. Thus, India will be conducting a new census after a gap of 16 years from 2011 to 2027. Personal Reflection: I still remember that good old day of February 2011, when a census worker came to our house and collected information from my grandfather. I was very young at that time and was observing everything quietly from a corner, seeing what was happening.

Importance of the 2027 Census:

Since I was born before 2011, I was fully included in this census. However, since a census has not been conducted for more than 15 years, many children born after February 2011 are not included in the existing official datasets. In the upcoming census, however, they will be included.
As of today, 26 May 2026, the Census 2011 still remains the latest official census of India and the latest official dataset. It will continue to remain the latest until the census is successfully completed in February 2027.
A census conducted in 2027 after such a long gap of 16 years would help the government by providing updated population, social, and economic data. This would be essential for accurately planning policies, allocating resources, and adjusting development programs according to current realities.
