The crown completes 180 years

16/03/2026 5:50 PM IST
@NiralBhatt

Jammu and Kashmir, often referred to as the “Crown of India”, completes 180 years today. 180 years ago, on 16 March 1846, the Treaty of Amritsar was signed between Maharaja Gulab Singh and the British, which created the state with the exact name, "Jammu and Kashmir", in a subsidiary alliance with British India under British India (under the British East India Company) until 1858, and thereafter under the suzerainty of British India (directly under the British Crown) from 1858 until the British withdrawal and the partition of British India in 1947, when it acceded to India.


This Indian first-tier administrative unit has maintained the exact same name for the longest time and still uses it today.
