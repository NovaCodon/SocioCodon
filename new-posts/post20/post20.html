My heritage explained

30/06/2026 01:28 PM IST
Niral Bhatt

Hi guys! I'm Niral Bhatt. I'm of Kashmiri Pandit descent, but I now live in Delhi. I want to give you a brief description of my heritage. It's actually quite confusing and complex, but I'll explain it.

My ancestors originally came from the Anantnag region of the Kashmir Valley. However, due to the unrest in the Kashmir Valley, they migrated southwards. Many settled in Jammu (directly south), while others went further south to regions such as Punjab (southwest) and the Central Himalayas (southeast). This brought about significant cultural changes within our ancestry. Many began identifying themselves as as Punjabi, while others identified themselves of the Central Himalayan region.

Around a century ago, most of the ancestors that I know of migrated to Delhi in for a better lifestyle. Past few generations of my family were born and raised in Delhi. Since then, our culture has been influenced more by modern urban life than by the culture of any particular region. As a result, I am culturally a typical Delhiite.
Many of my relatives either don't know about their Kashmiri ancestry, or, even if they do, they don't acknowledge it. Personally, I believe that the places our ancestors migrated to should not replace our ancestral homeland. Therefore, I think acknowledging the place from which we originally hail is the most accurate way to define our heritage. Many members of my family also support this view.

Therefore, the way I define my heritage is by calling myself a Delhiite of Kashmiri Pandit descent.
